Union Democratization in the Mexican Automobile Industry: A Reappraisal

Year: 1989  DOI ↗

Main topic: political science

Abstract
The Mexican automobile manufacturing industry experienced rapid sociopolitical change in the 1960s and 1970s as workers in several firms overthrew entrenched labor leaders and instituted democratic forms of union governance. These reform movements sought increased participation by the rank and file in union affairs and heightened worker control over different aspects of the production process.
